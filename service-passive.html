What Is Passive Fire Protection?

Passive fire protection (PFP) refers to the materials and construction methods designed to prevent the spread of fire and maintain the structural integrity of buildings. Unlike active systems like alarms or sprinklers, passive fire protection is always working in the background ‚Äî built into walls, ceilings, ducts, and joints.

Our Passive Fire Protection Solutions

  • Fire-Rated Walls & Partitions
    Compartmentalize buildings to restrict fire and smoke movement.
  • Firestop Systems
    Seal openings and penetrations in walls and floors to block fire spread.
  • Fire-Rated Ductwork & Dampers
    Maintain air control systems without compromising fire resistance.
  • Structural Steel Protection
    Apply fire-resistant coatings to beams and columns to delay structural failure.
  • Cable Trays & Service Penetrations
    Fireproofing around cable runs to keep critical systems protected.
